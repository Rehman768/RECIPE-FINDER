Ingredients

  • 500g basmati rice
  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Requirements

  • Large pot
  • Skillet
  • Spatula
  • Knife
  • Cutting board

Description

  1. Cook basmati rice until 70% done and set aside. This ensures the rice will finish cooking with the chicken and absorb all the flavors.
  2. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onions, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste. Saut√© until onions are golden brown. This forms the base of the biryani and adds depth of flavor.
  3. Add ground cumin, ground coriander, turmeric, and chili powder. Cook for 2 minutes to release the spices' aromas.
  4. Stir in tomatoes and cook for 10 minutes until they break down and form a thick sauce.
  5. Add chicken pieces and cook until chicken is no longer pink. This ensures the chicken is cooked through and absorbs the spices.
  6. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture in a large pot. Cover and cook on low heat until rice is fully cooked. This step allows the rice to steam and absorb the flavors from the chicken and spices.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with raita. The raita adds a cooling element to balance the spices in the biryani.
