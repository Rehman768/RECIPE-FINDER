Chole Bhature

Ingredients

  • 2 cups chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Requirements

  • Pressure cooker
  • Large pot
  • Spatula
  • Knife
  • Cutting board

Description

  1. Pressure cook soaked chickpeas with salt and water until tender.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onions, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste. Saut√© until onions are golden brown.
  3. Add ground cumin, ground coriander, turmeric, and chili powder. Cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Stir in tomato puree and cook for 10 minutes.
  5. Add cooked chickpeas and simmer for another 10 minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with bhature.

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish that is loved by many. It is a combination of spicy chickpeas (chole) and deep-fried bread (bhature). The chickpeas are cooked in a flavorful tomato-based gravy with a blend of spices, making it a delicious and hearty meal. The bhature is made from a dough of all-purpose flour, yogurt, and a pinch of baking soda, which is then rolled out and deep-fried until golden and puffed up. This dish is often enjoyed as a breakfast or lunch option and is usually served with pickles, onions, and a side of yogurt. It is a perfect dish for special occasions and gatherings, and its rich flavors and textures make it a favorite among all age groups.
