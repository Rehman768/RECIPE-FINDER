Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Requirements

  • Large pot
  • Skillet
  • Baking dish
  • Spatula
  • Knife
  • Cutting board

Description

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). This ensures the oven is hot enough to cook the lasagna evenly.
  2. Cook lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside. The noodles should be al dente.
  3. In a skillet, cook ground beef, onion, and garlic over medium heat until beef is browned. Drain excess fat. This forms the meat sauce base.
  4. Stir in marinara sauce and simmer for 10 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld together.
  5. In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, parsley, salt, and pepper. This forms the creamy cheese layer.
  6. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a baking dish. Layer with lasagna noodles, ricotta mixture, mozzarella cheese, and meat sauce. Repeat layers, ending with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese on top. This layering ensures each bite has a mix of flavors.
  7. Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until cheese is bubbly and golden. This ensures the lasagna is cooked through and the cheese is melted.
  8. Let stand for 10 minutes before serving. This allows the lasagna to set and makes it easier to slice.
