BBQ Ribs

Ingredients

  • 2 racks baby back ribs
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp salt

Requirements

  • Grill or oven
  • Aluminum foil
  • Brush
  • Knife
  • Cutting board

Description

  1. Preheat grill or oven to 300°F (150°C). This ensures the ribs cook slowly and evenly.
  2. In a small bowl, mix brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, and salt. This forms the dry rub for the ribs.
  3. Rub the spice mixture evenly over both sides of the ribs. This adds flavor to the meat.
  4. Wrap each rack of ribs in aluminum foil and place on the grill or in the oven. Cook for 2.5 to 3 hours, or until ribs are tender. This slow cooking process makes the ribs tender and juicy.
  5. Remove ribs from foil and brush with BBQ sauce. This adds a sweet and tangy flavor to the ribs.
  6. Place ribs back on the grill or in the oven and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until sauce is caramelized. This step adds a nice glaze to the ribs.
  7. Let rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve hot. Resting the ribs allows the juices to redistribute, making them more flavorful.
