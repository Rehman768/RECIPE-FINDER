Kung Pao Chicken

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken breast, cut into cubes
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tsp crushed red pepper flakes
  • Salt to taste

Requirements

  • Large mixing bowl
  • Skillet or wok
  • Spatula
  • Knife
  • Cutting board

Description

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, and cornstarch. Add chicken cubes and marinate for at least 30 minutes. This helps to tenderize the chicken and infuse it with flavor.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add marinated chicken and cook until browned. Remove chicken from skillet and set aside. This ensures the chicken is cooked through and has a nice sear.
  3. In the same skillet, add bell peppers, garlic, and ginger. Saut√© for 2-3 minutes until fragrant. This adds a depth of flavor to the dish.
  4. Return chicken to the skillet. Add roasted peanuts, green onions, and crushed red pepper flakes. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes. The peanuts add a crunchy texture, and the red pepper flakes add a bit of heat.
  5. Season with salt to taste. Serve hot with steamed rice. The rice helps to balance the flavors and makes it a complete meal.
